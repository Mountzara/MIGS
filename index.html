About MIGS

Minimally Invasive Gynecologic Surgery (MIGS) refers to surgical techniques that allow gynecologic surgeons to perform procedures through small incisions or natural orifices, reducing recovery time and improving patient outcomes.

MIGS encompasses a range of techniques including laparoscopy, hysteroscopy, robotic-assisted surgery, and vaginal approaches that minimize tissue trauma while maintaining or improving surgical outcomes.

Common Procedures

Laparoscopic Hysterectomy

Removal of the uterus through small abdominal incisions using a camera and specialized instruments.

Robotic Myomectomy

Robot-assisted removal of uterine fibroids while preserving the uterus.

Hysteroscopic Procedures

Treatment of intrauterine pathology through the natural cervical opening.

Endometriosis Excision

Removal of endometriotic lesions using minimally invasive techniques.
